About Nike

Nike, founded in 1964 as Blue Ribbon Sports by Bill Bowerman and Phil Knight, has grown into a global icon in athletic footwear and apparel. Renamed Nike, after the Greek goddess of victory, in 1971, the company revolutionized the sportswear industry with innovative designs and marketing strategies. The iconic "Swoosh" logo, designed by Carolyn Davidson, symbolizes motion and speed, becoming one of the most recognized logos worldwide.

Nike's success is attributed to its relentless focus on innovation. The introduction of the Air Max technology in the 1980s and the subsequent launch of performance-enhancing footwear solidified its position as a leader in sports technology. Collaborations with top athletes, including Michael Jordan, whose Air Jordan line remains immensely popular, have further cemented Nike’s reputation.
